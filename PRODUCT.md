# DreamCatcher — Product Definition

## Scope contract

- **Purpose:** Define the current product: who it is for, what it does, and what emotional/strategic outcomes matter.
- **Contains:** Core product loop, audience, competitive problem, device strategy, Sheepy definition, and strategic ambition.
- **Does not contain:** Marketing claims ladder, Insight schema/prompt rules, volatile branch state, or historical evolution dumps.
- **Update when:** Product definition, audience framing, Sheepy role, or core loop intent changes with acceptance.
- **Default loading behavior:** Load for product, UX, copy, Sheepy, scope, or priority decisions.

---

## Core product

DreamCatcher is a mobile-first dream-capture, interpretation, and reflection product.

- Users save dreams.
- Every remembered dream becomes a star in Sheepy’s sky.
- Sheepy keeps remembered dreams safe and helps users understand and reflect on them.
- Sheepy is the emotional companion and central product identity.

**Primary audience:** young adult women in the United States and other higher-purchasing-power markets, framed respectfully around journaling, interpretation, and self-reflection.

Avoid medical and diagnostic claims.

---

## Competitive problem

A user can open a general frontier model, create a dream journal, and request interpretations.

DreamCatcher must win through:

- lower recurring effort
- specialized Insight quality
- longitudinal continuity
- Sheepy
- emotional reward
- repeat-use ritual

---

## Device strategy

- Desktop/web may be stronger for beautiful acquisition and Reddit marketing.
- Mobile is stronger for retention because dreams are captured in bed or after waking.
- Desktop can drive acquisition.
- Mobile must drive retention.
- Mobile cannot feel inferior.

---

## Desired emotional outcomes

### After saving

The user should feel:

- the fragile dream was caught before disappearing
- it became a star or small light
- Sheepy is keeping it safe
- relief, care, and small wonder

### After Insight

The user should feel:

- Sheepy noticed a connection the user had not fully seen
- recognition, surprise, emotional accuracy, or meaningful self-understanding
- not diagnosis or scientifically certain interpretation

---

## Sheepy — canonical system definition

> Sheepy is DreamCatcher’s specialized dream intelligence: a user-facing interpretation system built around a frontier model and differentiated by DreamCatcher’s context, constraints, schema, continuity, evaluation harness, persistence, and emotional experience.

Clarify:

- Users may experience Sheepy as DreamCatcher’s specialized dream AI.
- DreamCatcher does not currently claim to have trained a proprietary foundation model.
- Interpretation remains the stronger consumer category and promise.
- Reflection describes the responsible method.
- Interpretations must not be presented as diagnostic or scientifically certain.
- Emotion is the hook; interpretation depth is the substance.
- Never accept an Insight regression merely to improve emotional presentation.

Public claims and proof requirements live in [MARKETING.md](./MARKETING.md). Insight quality doctrine lives in [INSIGHT_SYSTEM.md](./INSIGHT_SYSTEM.md).

---

## Strategic ambition

DreamCatcher aims to build and prove Sheepy as a superior default dream-interpretation experience compared with general frontier models and major dream apps.

This is an ambition and testable hypothesis, not a current public superiority claim.

---

## Protected copy candidates

These are high-value in-app candidates (home, return, save rituals—not necessarily onboarding). Do not casually rewrite, remove, or deploy all at once. Timing and context matter.

- “This is Sheepy. He tends the place where forgotten dreams go.”
- “Sheepy missed you.”
- “The night sky has been quiet without you.”
- “Sheepy kept your cloud warm.” — only when the user’s cloud is visually and conceptually established
- “It has been a while. Did any dreams find you?”
- “My dreams help keep this place alive.” — central concept; decide speaker (user / Sheepy / interface) before production placement
- “Your dream became a star.”

Copy should sound natural, emotional, clear, and alive—not passive or mechanical.
